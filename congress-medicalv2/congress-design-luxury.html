Campus Healthcare Crisis: An Analysis of Lack of Access and Availability Among College Populations

Abstract Body

Background: College is a challenging transitional stage as youth must independently manage their health. Most colleges require proof of health insurance before matriculation and offer affiliated insurances. Therefore, with many students shifting physicians secondary to aging out, insurance change, and geographical positions, they must now rely on campus health facilities (CHF). This study examines the availability and accessibility of CHFs to determine whether they provide the care needed by these young adults.

Methods: The top 3 most enrolled colleges in each of the 50 US states were identified by CollegeFactual and College Raptor. The following data were collected from each college and CHF website: 1) student population (SP); 2) number of clinical staff (CS) (MD/DO, NP, and PA); 3) CHF hours, # days open/week, weekend availability; 4) reviews on appointment availability (on Google or Reddit); 5) insurance information; and 6) services available. Analysis was done in R at an α = .05.
